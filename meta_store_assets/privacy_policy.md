# Privacy Policy for Horizon Stream

**Effective Date:** July 8, 2026

Horizon Stream ("the App") is an open-source, third-party PlayStation Remote Play client designed for Meta Quest devices. We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring transparency about how the App operates.

## Data Collection and Usage
**We do not collect, store, or transmit any of your personal information.** 

Horizon Stream operates entirely on your local network (or over a direct connection to your PlayStation console if configured via port forwarding). 
* The App **does not** use third-party analytics, tracking software, or crash reporting tools that send data to us or any external servers.
* The App **does not** communicate with our servers, because we do not operate any servers.
* All connections are established directly between your Meta Quest headset and your PlayStation console.

## PlayStation Network Account (PSN ID)
To pair your PlayStation console with the App, you are required to provide a Base64-encoded PSN Account ID. 
* This ID is stored **locally** on your Meta Quest device.
* It is used **exclusively** to authenticate and establish a direct Remote Play connection with your PlayStation console.
* It is **never** transmitted to us, to Meta, or to any third party.

## Device Permissions
The App may request the following permissions on your Meta Quest headset to function properly:
* **Network Access (Wi-Fi/Internet):** Required to discover your PlayStation console on the local network and to stream audio/video data.
* **Microphone (Optional):** Required only if you choose to use voice chat features during Remote Play.
* **Bluetooth (Optional):** Required to connect and use external wireless gamepads (e.g., DualSense or DualShock 4).

## Open Source Transparency
Horizon Stream is distributed under the GNU Affero General Public License v3.0 (AGPLv3). The entire source code of the application is publicly available. Anyone can review the code to verify that no hidden tracking, data collection, or unauthorized data transmission occurs.

## Contact Us
Because Horizon Stream is an open-source project maintained by the community, we do not have a dedicated support team for privacy inquiries. However, if you have questions or concerns about the code, you are welcome to open an issue or start a discussion on our public source code repository.
